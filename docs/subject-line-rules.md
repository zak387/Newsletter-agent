# Subject Line Rules

These rules apply to every newsletter subject line generated by the writing agent. They override any voice-profile-level guidance — voice is for body copy, these rules are for subject lines.

## Hard constraints

- **Target: 20 characters per subject line. Hard ceiling: 30 characters.** Including spaces and punctuation. Aim for 20 or under; never exceed 30.
- **Why:** the full line must show on mobile preview. Most readers are on mobile. If the subject line is cut off, open rates drop. 20 is the safe zone on most clients; 30 is the point past which most mobile previews will truncate.

## Frameworks

Every subject line must fit exactly one of these five frameworks:

| Framework | What it does | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Curiosity** | Creates an open loop the reader needs to close. | "This changed everything" |
| **Pain** | Surfaces a problem the reader already has and hasn't solved. | "Still can't convert traffic?" |
| **Dream Outcome** | Promises a specific, desirable result. | "2X your leads in 30 days" |
| **Proof** | Leads with a result you have achieved. | "How I grew to 70K followers" |
| **Mistake** | Exposes a costly error readers are likely making. | "I burnt $15K on this mistake" |

Note: several of the "example" subject lines above exceed 20 characters. They illustrate the *pattern*, not the length. Your generated lines must still be ≤20 characters.

## What to apply

- Tag every generated subject line with exactly one framework name from the five above.
- Reject any option that exceeds 20 characters before returning it to the operator.
- Aim for a spread across frameworks — do not return 15 Curiosity options.
