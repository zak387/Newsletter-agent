## The WHO put aspartame on the cancer list

- **Headline:** The WHO put aspartame on the cancer list [link]
- **TL;DR:** The WHO's cancer agency flagged aspartame as a possible cancer risk.

**Three things to know**
- Aspartame is the sweetener in most diet sodas, sugar-free gum, and the yogurts labeled light.
- The agency did not say aspartame causes cancer. It said the evidence is there and it is worth flagging. That is a signal, not a sentence.
- The fix is not another artificial sweetener. The fix is out of the category.

**My take**

I was not buying aspartame before this. I am still not. One diet soda is not going to hurt you. The question is whether you want it in your cart every week for the next ten years. That is the only question.

Most of the diet sodas you used to buy now have a monk fruit or allulose version on the same shelf. Flip both cans over. The front of the can is a sales pitch. The back is a contract. Read the contract and pick the better one.

## Erythritol just got flagged for heart problems

- **Headline:** Erythritol just got flagged for heart problems [link]
- **TL;DR:** The Cleveland Clinic tied erythritol in the bloodstream to higher rates of heart attack and stroke.

**Three things to know**
- Erythritol is the bulk sweetener in most stevia and monk fruit products on the shelf. Read the back. If your monk fruit sweetener is ninety-nine percent erythritol, you are buying erythritol.
- It is not proof. It is a strong signal in a category sold as the safe one.
- It does not change the verdict on real monk fruit or real stevia leaf. It changes the verdict on the blends.

**My take**

This one caught me. I used erythritol for years. I told other people to use it. I am not doing either of those anymore.

Not because one cookie is going to kill me. Because when a signal this big shows up in a sweetener hiding in every clean-looking product on the shelf, the move is to flip the bag and find the version without it. Lakanto makes a monk fruit line that is just monk fruit. That is the one I grab now.

## Allulose is the first real sugar swap in ten years

- **Headline:** Allulose is the first real sugar swap in ten years [link]
- **TL;DR:** The FDA gave allulose its own line on the nutrition label and it is showing up across the store.

**Three things to know**
- Allulose tastes like sugar. Bakes like sugar. Your body does not process it like sugar. Almost no blood sugar hit.
- A product can say zero added sugars on the label when allulose is the sweetener. That is a real rule, not a loophole.
- Brands worth knowing: RxSugar, Magic Spoon, some Quest lines, some Rebel ice creams.

**My take**

This is the best thing to happen in the sweetener aisle in ten years. Allulose is the closest thing on the shelf to real sugar that does not act like real sugar in your body.

If you bake, get RxSugar. If you buy protein bars, flip the bar over and look for allulose instead of sucralose. The swap is already on the shelf. Your cart can catch up this week.
