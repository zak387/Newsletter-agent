Eight out of ten products have seed oils. Here is how to spot them in ten seconds.

Kyle here.

Eighty percent of what is on the shelf right now has seed oils in it. That includes the stuff marketed to families like yours, with clean fonts and leafy packaging and words like wholesome on the front.

The front of the package is a sales pitch. The back is a contract. Most people only read the pitch.

Here is the ten-second version of reading the contract.

Now the ten-second version.

There are eight oils I want you to memorise. Six words, really.

Canola. Soybean. Corn. Sunflower. Safflower. Cottonseed. Grapeseed. Rice bran.

That is it. Those are the seed oils. When you flip a product over and scan the ingredients, you are looking for any of those eight. If one of them is in the first five ingredients, put it back.

That is the whole skill.

Most people assume the healthy brands are fine. They are not. Most of them use the cheaper seed oils because seed oils are cheaper than olive oil, avocado oil, coconut oil, beef tallow, or butter. Branding is expensive. Ingredients are where the corners get cut.

Organic does not fix this either. Organic canola oil is still canola oil. You can have organic Doritos. Organic is a farming certification, not a seed oil exemption.

Here is what I do.

I pick up the product. I flip it over. I do not read every ingredient. I scan for the six words: canola, soybean, corn, sunflower, safflower, cottonseed. Grapeseed and rice bran are rarer but worth learning. If any of those words appear in the first five ingredients, the product goes back on the shelf. No deliberation, no bargaining, no reading the rest.

Ten seconds.

You will be surprised how fast you get at this. After a week of grocery shopping this way, you will know the brands in your categories that are safe and the brands that are not. You will stop picking things up that used to look fine. The decision gets easier every trip.

A few swaps that will cover most of your cart:

Cooking oil: replace with olive oil, avocado oil, beef tallow, or butter.

Crackers: look for ones made with olive oil or avocado oil. Simple Mills and Mary's Gone Crackers both have lines that work.

Salad dressing: almost every mainstream bottle is a seed oil product. Primal Kitchen makes avocado oil versions at most grocery stores now, including Walmart.

Mayo: same story. Primal Kitchen at most stores.

Chips: Siete uses avocado oil. Some Jackson's Honest lines too.

Frozen meals: harder. Most of them are seed oil heavy. This is where the Playbook earns its keep.

Start there. Three swaps this week. Three reads at the grocery store. By next weekend the skill is in your hands and the six words are memorised. You will not need me for this part anymore.

Progress over perfection. You are not going to catch every seed oil on the first trip, and you do not need to. What you need is the scan, the six words, and the habit of flipping the package over before anything goes in the cart.

That is week one.
