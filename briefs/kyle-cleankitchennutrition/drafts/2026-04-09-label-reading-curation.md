## Label Reading — The One Skill That Replaces Every Protocol

Date: 2026-04-09

Subject Line: Eat almost anything, safely

---

## 3 Ideas

1. The average person makes 200 food decisions a day. When you can read a label in ten seconds, 180 of them stop being hard. The other 20 stop being guesses.

2. The food industry did not invent new foods. They invented new names for old ones. Added sugar hides under 56 different names on labels you already shop. Refined wheat hides behind "whole grain." Seed oils hide inside "vegetable oil." Learning the aliases is the whole job.

3. You do not have to be 100 percent clean to be healthy. You have to be 100 percent literate. The 20 percent that is not perfect is fine. The part that matters is that you know what you are eating and why, not that you ate it.

## 2 Quotes

> "This is the first study to demonstrate causality — that ultra-processed foods cause people to eat too many calories and gain weight."
> — Kevin Hall, PhD, NIH lead researcher (Cell Metabolism, 2019)

> "Although the FDA has not engaged in rulemaking to establish a formal definition for the term 'natural,' the agency does have a longstanding policy concerning the use of 'natural' in human food labeling."
> — U.S. Food and Drug Administration, "Use of the Term Natural on Food Labeling"
