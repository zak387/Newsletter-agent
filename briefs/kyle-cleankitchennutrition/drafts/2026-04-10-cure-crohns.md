## I had Crohn's for six years before I read a single label

I got diagnosed at 22. Spent the next six years on medication, managing flares, and accepting that this was my life. No doctor ever asked me what I was eating. Not once.

Then I started reading ingredients. Not nutrition facts. Ingredients.

Within four months my inflammation markers were normal. Within a year I was off medication. My GI doctor used the word "remission." I use the word "fixed."

I am not telling you to stop your meds. I am telling you what happened to me when I stopped eating things my body could not recognize as food.

## The standard advice keeps people sick

"Eat bland food during flares." So you survive on white rice and applesauce made with high fructose corn syrup.

"Avoid fiber." So you remove the one thing that feeds beneficial gut bacteria.

"It's genetic, you just manage it." So you never look at the actual inputs going into your system daily.

"Try an elimination diet." So you cut out 40 foods for 8 weeks, reintroduce them wrong, learn nothing, and quit.

None of this addresses the real problem: most packaged food contains emulsifiers, seed oils, and synthetic additives that damage intestinal lining in people who are already inflamed.

## Read the back of the package, not the front

My body was not reacting to food. It was reacting to ingredients pretending to be food.

Polysorbate 80. Carrageenan. Soy lecithin. Maltodextrin. These are in "health foods." They are in protein bars marketed to people with gut issues. They are in the broth your doctor told you to sip during a flare.

The fix is not a protocol. The fix is literacy.

Three things I did that moved the needle more than six years of medication:

1. Removed every product from my kitchen that contained an emulsifier or seed oil. Took one afternoon.
2. Replaced those products with single-ingredient versions. Butter instead of margarine. Olive oil instead of canola. Whole fruit instead of fruit snacks.
3. Gave my gut 90 days of clean inputs before judging whether anything changed.

It changed.

## Quick answers to your questions this week

**Protein goals:** You do not need 1g per pound of bodyweight unless you are actively building muscle. For most people eating real food, 0.7g per pound is plenty. Get it from eggs, beef, full-fat dairy. Stop relying on protein powder with 15 ingredients.

**Fasting:** I do not fast on a schedule. I eat when I am hungry and stop when I am not. If you want a rule, stop eating 3 hours before bed. That alone fixes most bloating and reflux issues people email me about.

**Gut healing timeline:** 90 days minimum of consistent clean eating before you evaluate. Your gut lining turns over every 3-4 days but the microbiome takes months to shift. Be patient. Progress over perfection.

## Food should not feel like a minefield

It does not have to be complicated. Read labels. Remove the worst offenders. Give your body time. That is the entire framework.
