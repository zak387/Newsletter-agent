## The clean label movement is no longer niche

Eight years ago I was standing in a Walmart flipping over protein bars, Googling ingredient names on my phone while people stared. That version of me would not believe what is happening right now.

## What I have been saying since 2018 is now federal policy

The clean label movement went mainstream. Not trending, not buzzy. Mainstream. Fifty-one percent of consumers now actively seek clean label products. Louisiana passed a bill targeting 40+ ingredients including seed oils and synthetic dyes. The new US Dietary Guidelines call out highly processed foods by name for the first time. The FDA is reforming the GRAS pathway that let questionable ingredients slide through for decades.

The market for clean label products is projected to hit $212 billion by 2035.

I started reading labels in a gastroenterologist's office in 2018 because my Crohn's was destroying me and my doctors told me diet had nothing to do with it. I stopped listening to them and started reading the back of every package I picked up. Seed oils in everything. Emulsifiers in products labeled natural. Refined wheat flour hiding behind whole grain claims.

My body was not reacting to food. It was reacting to ingredients pretending to be food.

That was not a popular opinion in 2018. It is a billion-dollar industry in 2026.

## What this means at the grocery store

Brands are racing to remove seed oils. Restaurants are switching to beef tallow. Companies that built their business on "natural" labels with garbage ingredient lists are scrambling to reformulate. The front of the package is a sales pitch. The back is a contract. More people are finally reading the contract.

Some of the shift is genuine reformulation. The rest is the same companies slapping new labels on old products because demand changed. That is exactly why reading ingredients still matters more than reading marketing.

## Three things to do this week

1. Pick up the cooking oil in your kitchen. If it says vegetable, canola, soybean, or sunflower oil, replace it with avocado oil or butter. That single swap removes the most common inflammatory ingredient in your house.

2. Check the bread in your pantry. If the first ingredient is enriched wheat flour, that is refined flour with a better name. Look for whole wheat flour or sprouted wheat as the first ingredient.

3. Next time a brand says "clean" or "natural" on the front, flip it over. Read the back. If you cannot pronounce three or more ingredients, the front lied to you.

Progress over perfection.
