## Niche Umbrella

**Personal transformation > gut healing + healthy eating**

Health-conscious everyday grocery shoppers who want to eat freely without fear or confusion and heal their gut. The sub-niche sits at the intersection of gut health recovery and practical clean eating — not an ideology, a way of living.

---

## Target Reader

A person in their late 20s to early 40s who has started paying attention to what they eat after a health scare, persistent gut issues, low energy, or a growing distrust of processed food. They want to eat cleaner but feel paralysed at the grocery store — health claims contradict each other, ingredient lists are unreadable, and every product seems to market itself as healthy. They have tried strict diets before and burned out. They are not looking for a new set of rules or another ideology to follow. They want a practical, non-punishing framework they can apply on a normal week, with normal time and a normal budget. They follow health content but struggle to translate it into consistent real-world choices.

---

## Newsletter Statement

This newsletter helps health-conscious people achieve confidence in what they put in their bodies by decoding food labels, cutting through the tribalism of diet culture, and delivering a practical, balanced clean-eating framework built from one person's experience managing a chronic illness through food.

---

## Why Does This Content Need to Exist?

The clean label movement is no longer niche — 51% of consumers now actively seek clean label products, and the global clean-label ingredients market is projected to reach $212 billion by 2035. Yet the information gap is widening: food labelling has become more sophisticated and deliberately misleading, not less. At the same time, diet culture has fractured into competing dogmas — keto versus vegan versus carnivore — that leave the majority of health-curious people without a practical middle path. No major independent newsletter occupies the specific intersection of chronic illness recovery, ingredient transparency, and a balanced, non-ideological approach to clean eating.

## Why This Creator Specifically?

Kyle was diagnosed with Crohn's Disease in 2018 and was told by his gastroenterologist that diet had no meaningful role in managing his condition. He rejected that and spent three years independently researching nutrition, ultimately managing his disease through food. That is not a wellness glow-up story — it is a documented, high-stakes result with a named medical condition. His podcast, the Clean Kitchen Podcast, gives him direct access to founders and decision-makers inside the food and beverage industry — a credibility layer most health creators do not have. He understands how food products are made and marketed from the inside, which sharpens his ingredient evaluations beyond surface-level label reading.

## Top 3 Content Pillars

### 1. Label School
What readers get: the knowledge to decode any food label in under 60 seconds — spotting hidden additives, misleading health claims, and ingredient red flags across every category from snacks to supplements.
How it moves them: eliminates the paralysis of the grocery aisle and builds a transferable skill they use on every shop, not just the ones they plan for.

### 2. The Clean Swap
What readers get: specific, research-backed product swaps that replace the processed versions of everyday staples — the exact alternatives Kyle has personally evaluated against clean criteria.
How it moves them: reduces friction between wanting to eat clean and actually doing it. No ideology required, just better defaults.

### 3. Balance Over Perfection
What readers get: a consistent counter-narrative to the rigidity of diet culture — content that frames clean eating as a sustainable practice built on judgment and flexibility, not rules and restriction.
How it moves them: addresses the primary reason people abandon clean eating: the all-or-nothing trap. Readers who feel like they can succeed without perfection stay in the game longer.

## Primary Business Model

**Backend coaching**

Kyle has an existing email list of ~15,000 subscribers. The newsletter's primary job is to deepen trust and move qualified readers into a coaching offer — not to build a list from scratch. Affiliates and sponsorships remain secondary revenue, but the monetisation engine is a coaching programme sold to the warmest segment of the list. At this list size, even a small conversion rate into a high-ticket coaching offer produces meaningful revenue without requiring scale.

## Competitor Insight

The newsletter landscape in clean eating and personal nutrition is thin at the creator level. **Dan Go** (505K subscribers) owns the body transformation and performance lane for entrepreneurs — well-positioned but not overlapping. **Elizabeth Rider** covers clean eating on Substack but is recipe and lifestyle-forward rather than ingredient education. **Nest Wellness** and similar publications serve a dietary-restriction-specific readership. **Clean Eating Magazine** is legacy media with none of the personal authority or specificity that makes creator newsletters convert. The white space Kyle can own: a creator-led, balanced, Crohn's-origin, ingredient-transparency newsletter that teaches people how to shop and think about food — without the dogma.
