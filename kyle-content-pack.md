### EMAIL 3: February Monthly Gift -- 6 Food Myths Debunked

Kyle here. Happy February.

This month I started something new: a monthly gift. No sales pitch. No product launch. Genuinely useful information you can apply immediately.

The average person makes 200 food-related decisions every single day. When you are operating on bad information, myths that sound true but are not, those 200 decisions become even harder.

MYTH 1 -- Low-Fat Foods Are Better for You

This myth came from cherry-picked studies in the 1960s and 70s that convinced the FDA and USDA that fat was the enemy. The data was flawed. Researchers had a conclusion they wanted, then worked backward.

Fat is not the enemy. Good fats from avocados, grass-fed butter, beef tallow, olive oil, and eggs are satiating, essential for hormone regulation, critical for brain health, and important for gut health.

When I switched from primarily eating chicken to eating more beef, I stopped snacking constantly. I was not hungry two hours after eating.

Always choose full-fat dairy. Cook with butter, ghee, or beef tallow instead of seed oils. Do not fear fat. Seek it out.

MYTH 2 -- If It Says Organic, It Is Healthy

Organic means no synthetic pesticides, antibiotics, hormones, or chemical processing. That matters. But organic does not mean healthy. You can have organic Doritos. Still junk, just expensive junk.

Use organic as one filter, not the only filter. Always check the ingredients. For produce with skin you eat, prioritize organic. For thick-skinned produce, it is less critical.

MYTH 3 -- All Calories Are Created Equal

This is the biggest lie in the fitness industry. 100 calories of eggs is not the same as 100 calories of Doritos. One egg contains all essential amino acids plus vitamins A, D, E, K, B12, folate, and choline. Doritos have empty calories. Protein has a higher thermic effect -- your body burns more calories digesting it. The egg fills you up. Doritos do not.

Recent studies show people on ultra-processed diets consume 500 more calories daily even with identical calorie availability. It is not about calories. It is about quality. Stop counting calories. Start reading ingredients.

MYTH 4 -- Carbs Are the Enemy

Refined carbs, enriched wheat flour, white bread, crackers, are bad. Unrefined carbs, fruit, vegetables, white rice, are fine. Your body needs carbs for energy and brain function.

Get your carbs from fruit, vegetables, and white rice. For bread, look for whole grain wheat flour on the ingredient list, not enriched. White rice is easier to digest than brown and has lower arsenic levels.

MYTH 5 -- Eating Healthy Is Too Expensive

Shop the outer aisles first: produce, meat, dairy. That should be 80 percent of your diet.

Affordable whole foods: bananas (six or seven for under a dollar), eggs, ground beef, white rice, frozen vegetables. Walmart has grass-fed beef for five to six dollars a pound. You do not need Whole Foods.

Prioritize real food over processed, even if it is conventional. Upgrade one thing at a time.

MYTH 6 -- You Have to Be Perfect to Be Healthy

I am a perfectionist. When I first got into healthy eating, I thought I had to be 100 percent perfect. That mindset stalled me. It made me stressed. And stress does more harm than eating an Oreo.

I used to refuse to eat out because I could not control the ingredients. I was miserable.

Now I eat out. I order a steak or a burger without the bun. Sometimes I eat fries cooked in seed oils. I do not freak out about it.

The 80/20 rule: 80 percent of the time, real high-quality food. 20 percent of the time, less-than-perfect food. You will be fine. Progress over perfection. Enjoy your life. Eat real food. Give yourself grace.

Kyle
